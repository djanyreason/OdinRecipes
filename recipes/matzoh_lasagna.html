Matzoh Lasagna

A traditional meat lasagna made with matzoh instead of lasagna noodles. Very tasty, and great for Chosen People who don't keep kosher but who do keep Passover kosher.

For baking, you will want a deep square oven-safe baking dish about the size of 1 sheet of matzoh (about 8.5"x8.5").

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. ground meat of your choice (suggestions: beef&pork mix, turkey)
  • 1 Tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 & 1/4 tsp. kosher salt
  • 3/8 tsp. black pepper
  • 2.5 cups marinara-based pasta sauce (I suggest something like tomato&basil or roasted garlic)
  • 1 Tbsp. Italian seasoning
  • 16oz. whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 3/4 cup shredded parmesean cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1.5 cups shredded mozarella cheese
  • 3 sheets matzoh

Instructions

  1. Pre-heat oven to 375 degrees fahrenheit. Heat olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add ground meat, 1 Tbsp. salt, and 1/4 tsp. pepper, and cook until browned. Add pasta sauce, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. While meat & sauce are simmering, in a large mixing bowl combine the italian seasoning, ricotta, egg, half of the parmesean cheese, and the remaining salt and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  3. In a medium mixing bowl, combine mozarella and remaining parmesean. Mix together until well combined.
  4. Add 1/3rd of meat sauce to baking dish, then place one sheet of matzoh above and gently press down - firmly enough that sauce will slightly soak through holes in matzoh but not hard enough to break the sheet of matzoh. Add 1/3rd of ricotta mixture above matzoh and spread evenly across the sheet of matzoh, covering the baking dish to its sides. Sprinkle 1/3rd of the mozarella/parmesean mixture evenly above the ricotta. Add 2 more layers to this dish in the same order - meat sauce, then matzoh, then ricotta, then cheese.
  5. Cover the lasagna with aluminum foil, and place in the oven on a rack in the middle. Bake for 50 minutes.
  6. After 50 minutes, remove the foil from the top of the lasagna. Cook for an additional 10 minutes in the oven, or until cheese on top is browned.
  7. Remove lasagna from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
