Æbleskiver

Æbleskiver are a traditional Danish breakfast treat - kind of like pancake balls. They are best served with jam or powdered sugar.

To cook æbleskiver you need a special pan, called an æbleskiver pan. You can find many for purchase online. I use the Nordic Ware Danish Ebelskiver Pan.

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 1/3rd cup buttermilk
  • 2/3rd cup flour
  • 1 tsp. sugar
  • 1/12th tsp. salt
  • 1/6th tsp. baking soda
  • 2 tbsp. melted butter
  • Jam or powdered sugar, to serve

Instructions

  1. Separate egg yolk and white. Set aside white for later.
  2. In a small mixing bowl, combine egg yolk, buttermilk, flour, sugar, salt, and baking soda. Mix together until well combined. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes to rise.
  3. Once the batter has risen, whip the egg whites until stiff. Fold gently into the batter.
  4. Heat the æbleskiver pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter into each æbleskiver pocket. Add batter to pockets until approximately 3/4ths full. When the dough at the bottom has formed a light brown crust but the top is still runny, gently but quickly flip the æbleskiver in the pocket using a skewer and/or silverware (I prefer to use a butter knife and a large soup spoon). Continue periodically turning over the æbleskiver until golden brown on all sides, about 5-6 minutes.
  5. Serve warm, with jam or confectioner's sugar.
